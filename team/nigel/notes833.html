Notes 8.3.3


Per-Connection Throughput - number of bytes per second at the reciever.
Offerred Load - Rate at which the transport layer sends segments into the network.
Choke Packet - Single bit denoting a router is congested.
Resource Management Cells - Convey congestion-related information among hosts and switches.
Explicit Forward Congestion Indication (EFCI) Bit - Single bit in all data cells that indicates congestion.
No Increase (NI) Bit - For mild congestion; don't increase data flow.
Explicit Rate (ER) Field - 2-bit value that denotes the minimum supportable rate on all switches in source-destination path.
Congestion Window (CWND) - Amount of unacknowledged data may not exceed min(cwnd,rwnd).
Self-Checking - TCP uses acks to trigger its increase in congested window size.
TCP Congestion Control Algorithm - Slow start, congestion avoidance, fast recovery.
TCP's congestion control is known as an Additive-Increase Multiplicative-Decrease (AIMD) form of congestion control.
