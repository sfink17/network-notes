When the network fails packets that cross that link may be lost. When the network heals, packets may cross shorter paths to get to the destination. Shorter paths have a delay-bandwidth product less than longer paths so can hold fewer bytes in the pipe. Using a TCP connection with a high traffic over a shorter path may also cause packet loss, since the shorter paths will get congested, and buffers may overflow causing multiple packet losses.
