Notes for October 10th

  • Modern Internet uses one of two transport protocols: TCP or UDP
  • Transmission Control Protocol
  • User Datagram Protocol
  • Network application deveoper chooses TCP or UDP based on the needs of the application
  • A packet in the transport layer is called a segment
  • (some things call UDP packets datagrams, but this textbook says nah that's too confusing)
  • TCP/UDP as a whole is called the Internet Protocol (IP)
  • Best-effort delivery serviece - makes every attempt to deliver segments, but makes no guarantees
  • Not guaranteed -> unreliable service
  • TCP/UDP extend IP's delivery service between two end systems to a delivery service between two processes on those systems
  • ^Transport-layer Multiplexing and Demultiplexing^
  • Integrity checking: include error-detection fields in segment headers
  • UDP
    • Only provides data delivery and error checking, no frills
      • Unreliable service - does not guarantee that data will arrive intact or at all
      • No congestion control - any connection can send as much as it wants for as long as it wants
  • TCP
    • Ensures reliable data transfer
      • Flow control, sequence numbers, acknowledgements, timers
      • Keeps sending data until all of it has been confirmed to arrive in the correct order
    • Provides congestion control
      • Prevents any one TCP connection from swamping links and routers with excessive traffic
      • Attempts to give each connection in a congested link an equal share of bandwith
      • Regulates rate at which sending sides of a TCP connection can send information
