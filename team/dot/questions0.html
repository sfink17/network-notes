1. What are the three major components of the Internet mail system?
    • User agents, mail servers, and the Simple Mail Transfer Protocol.
  2. Would a bank use TCP or UDP? Why?
    • A bank would use TCP to ensure that no data is lost in transmission.
  3. What does DNS stand for?
    • DNS stands for Domain Name System.
  4. What are the layers in a 7-layer protocol stack?
    1. Application
    2. Presentation
    3. Session
    4. Transport
    5. Network
    6. Link
    7. Physical
  5. What is a protocol?
    • A protocol is the format and order of messages exchanged between two or more entities and the actions taken upon transmission and/or receipt of a message or other event
  6. What does "assymetric" mean in terms of an ISP?
    • "Assymetric" means that data is being uploaded and downloaded at different rates. Generally, in residential ISPs, the download rate is higher than the upload rate.
  7. Name a disadvantage and advantage of using geosynchronous satellites for Internet access.
    • Disadvantage: high signal propagation delay. Advantage: can reach anywhere in the world, including areas difficult to connect with other physical media.
  8. What is a DoS attack? Explain how one works.
    • A Denial of Service attack overloads a server with messages, preventing other users from accessing it. There are three kinds of DoS attack.
      • Vulnerability attack: send a few well-crafted messages to a vulnerable application or operating system on target host. The 'right' packets can cause the service or host to crash.
      • Bandwidth flooding: send so many packets that the access link becomes clogged, preventing legitimate packages from reaching the server
      • Connection Flooding: open but never close enough TCP connections to prevent the server from opening connections with legitimate clients
